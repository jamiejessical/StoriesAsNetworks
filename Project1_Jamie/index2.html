The exclamation mark, also known as the exclamation point, has its origins in ancient Rome. It is believed to have evolved from the Latin word "io," which was an exclamation of joy or triumph. Over time, the "i" and the "o" merged into a single character, and this symbol eventually developed into the modern exclamation mark.

In medieval manuscripts, scribes would sometimes use the Latin word "nota" (meaning "note") above a line of text to indicate emphasis or excitement. This practice eventually led to the development of various punctuation marks, including the exclamation mark, to convey strong emotions and exclamatory statements.
